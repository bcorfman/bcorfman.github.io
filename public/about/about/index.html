“It is our job to create computer technology such that nobody has to program. And that the programming language is human.” - NVIDIA CEO Jensen Huang from an interview captured here.

How true is this right now? Is this type of artificial intelligence still being developed? I’m a computer scientist and a software developer, and I really enjoy writing code, but I also have limited time in my life. On the other hand I’m an ambitious guy, and I have several ideas for medium-sized software projects that I’d like to accomplish. Some of these projects are also more than just code, and they require all sorts of additional sound and graphics assets. What if AI (or a team of AIs) could help me write these projects while I act as a team lead? That’s what I’d like to find out. I’m blogging my investigation because I haven’t seen anyone really document this kind of research … working on an increasingly complex set of creative tasks that one or several developers or asset builders perform, but that I’m asking a Large Language Model (LLM) to do instead. I’m about to find out if the right amount of prompt engineering and directed tasks can result in decent software applications. I’ll start small and work my way up to see if and where the technology breaks. I’ll also be looking for comments from other developers to see if there are better methods than the ones I’m using, or if I’m on a path to success.
